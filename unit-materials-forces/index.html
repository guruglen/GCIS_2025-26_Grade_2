Grade 2 Newsletter: Designing Our World

Upcoming Unit: Properties and Forces

Dear Parents,

We are thrilled to begin our new unit of inquiry! This unit is all about discovery, testing, and building. We will be looking at the world around us through the eyes of scientists, mathematicians, and engineers.

Central Idea:
"The properties of materials determine how they respond to forces, and understanding this relationship allows us to design and build things that work."

🔬 Science: Materials and Forces

In this unit, students will become "Material Experts." We will explore:

  • Properties: Distinguishing between natural and manufactured materials and testing them to see which are strong, flexible, or waterproof.
  • Forces: Investigating how pushes and pulls can change how an object moves or even change its physical shape.
  • Energy & Light: A brief look at light sources, the nature of darkness, and the basics of how simple electricity circuits work.

📐 Mathematics: Geometry and Measurement

Our math focus will support our building projects as we master the following:

  • Shapes: Identifying and sketching 2D and 3D shapes by their properties (sides, vertices, and faces). We will also be hunting for symmetry in our designs!
  • Measurement: Learning to accurately measure and estimate length, mass, and capacity using standard units—essential skills for any young engineer.
  • Angles & Turns: Understanding turns (half, quarter, clockwise) and how objects look from different orientations.

📖 English Language: Fairy Tales and Fluency

Our literacy work will take a magical turn! We will be:

  • Reading: Developing fluency and expression by exploring fairy tales and non-fiction texts about how things are made.
  • Writing: Using the structure of familiar stories and poems to create our own narratives. We will also focus on expanding our vocabulary using prefixes and suffixes (like un-, dis-, and -ful).
  • Speaking: Practicing how to change our tone and vocabulary depending on who we are talking to.

🛠 The Final Outcome: The Fairy Tale Challenge

To pull all of this learning together, our students will face a creative engineering challenge! As they retell a classic fairy tale, they will identify a problem faced by a character and design and build a solution.

Whether it's building a bridge for the Three Billy Goats Gruff or a sturdy house for the Three Little Pigs, students will need to choose the right materials based on their properties and use their knowledge of shapes to make their design functional.

We can't wait to see what these young minds create! Thank you for your continued support.
